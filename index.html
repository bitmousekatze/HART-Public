HART Production Bible

How the system works · public overview · v1.0

What this is

HART is a public art platform built around a single token (hART) on Solana. This bible documents how the production systems behave — the rules, flows, and economic levers — without exposing keys, internal endpoints, or operational secrets. It's split into five chapters so each domain reads cleanly on its own.

Code, schema names, and pricing curves shown here describe intent. The live repo is the source of truth for current implementation; this bible is the source of truth for design.

Chapters

1. Community Canvas

Weekly 6900×6700 canvas, paint-by-burn, anti-grief curves, auction + 100% burn at week's end.

2. Gifting

Sending hART and artworks wallet-to-wallet. Receipts, attribution, and refusal flow.

3. Trading

Peer-to-peer artwork swaps. Escrow shape, atomicity, and dispute boundaries.

4. Buying & Selling

Listings, fixed-price + auction, royalties, fee model, and the burn relay.

5. Profile

Identity, display preferences, ownership history, attribution surface, privacy toggles.

Conventions used in this bible

  • RULE A hard system invariant — code enforces it.
  • DESIGN A deliberate product choice — could be tuned, but reasoned.
  • Money is always denominated in hART. Solana is the settlement layer.
  • "Burn" means an on-chain transfer to a publicly-known burn address — anyone can verify.
  • "Platform wallet" is a relay address. It does not custody for users; it batches burns.

Out of scope (intentionally)

  • Keys, RPC endpoints, service URLs, deploy targets — never published here.
  • Moderator identities and the internal mod console.
  • Exact rate limits, anti-abuse heuristics, and fraud signals.
  • Database connection details and row-level security policies (covered in private security docs).
