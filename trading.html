Trading

Peer-to-peer artwork swaps. Atomic, opt-in, no platform middle.

What a trade is

A trade is a two-sided, atomic exchange: each side offers items (artworks and/or hART), both sides sign, and the swap settles in a single transaction. Either side can walk away before signing; once both have signed, the trade either fully settles or fully reverts — never partial.

  • RULE Atomicity. Both legs settle together or neither does.
  • RULE Both wallets must sign. There is no unilateral trade.
  • DESIGN The platform does not arbitrate fairness. Trades are voluntary.

What can be on each side

An offer is a basket. Each side's basket may contain:

  • Zero or more artworks the offerer currently owns and that are not locked elsewhere.
  • An optional hART amount to balance the trade.

Empty baskets are allowed on at most one side (i.e. a "give" is a gift; see the gifting chapter — the system routes such cases through the gift flow instead).

Lifecycle

  1. PROPOSE Initiator builds a two-sided offer and sends it to the counterparty's inbox.
  2. REVIEW Counterparty inspects items, balances, and any on-chain conditions. Can counter, decline, or accept.
  3. COUNTER A counter is a new proposal that supersedes the previous one. Old proposal is voided.
  4. LOCK When both sides accept, all listed items are locked from other actions.
  5. SIGN Each side signs the swap transaction in their wallet.
  6. SETTLE Atomic on-chain swap. Ownership flips for all items in one tx.
  7. CANCEL Before final signing, either side can cancel — items unlock immediately.
  8. EXPIRE Proposals auto-expire if not acted on within the standard window.

Locking rules

Item stateEligible for trade?
Owned, idleYes
Listed for saleNo — owner must delist first
In another trade proposalNo — only one open trade at a time per item
Pending giftNo
Under moderation reviewNo
The single-open-trade-per-item rule prevents accidental double-commits. If you need to entertain multiple offers, use the auction flow under Buying & Selling.

Royalties & fees

  • RULE If a trade includes any hART on either side, royalties on the artwork(s) moving in the opposite direction are paid based on the hART leg.
  • RULE A pure artwork-for-artwork swap (no hART either side) is treated as a barter and pays no royalties.
  • DESIGN Bartering shouldn't be punished, but cash-balanced swaps are sales in disguise — they pay artists.
  • Platform fee on trades with hART is the same rate as on direct sales (see Buying & Selling).

Receipts & provenance

A settled trade writes one receipt visible to both sides and updates each artwork's provenance with a trade reason code. The receipt records:

  • Both wallets, full basket on each side, hART amounts
  • Royalties paid (if any) and to which creators
  • Settlement transaction signature
  • Optional message attached to the proposal

Disputes

Because trades are atomic and both-sides-signed, post-settlement disputes about what was swapped do not exist — the on-chain record is final. The only dispute surface is off-platform claims (e.g., "we agreed I'd add more hART later"). The platform does not enforce or arbitrate side agreements; users are advised to put all consideration inside the trade itself.

If you intend to receive payment "after" a transfer, that is a sale, not a trade — use the sale flow so the system handles escrow and royalty correctly.

Anti-abuse

  • Proposal sends are rate-limited per wallet to prevent inbox spam.
  • A counterparty who has blocked the sender will not receive proposals.
  • Mods can void a pending proposal that involves a flagged artwork; pending locks release.
  • Asset previews in the proposal are rendered from canonical, sanitized metadata only.
